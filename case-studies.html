GCSE Support

GCSE Maths: rebuilding confidence and closing core gaps.

A 14-week programme for a Year 11 student whose average topic test performance had dropped to 48%.

Before 48%

Average topic score

After 72%

Average topic score

Mock shift 5 to 7

Working grade movement

Challenge

The student was working hard but underperforming in GCSE Maths. Anxiety around unfamiliar questions, gaps in algebra and ratio, and weak confidence in multi-step reasoning were holding back performance.

Our approach

We carried out a diagnostic review of recent tests, topic fluency, and exam habits. A 14-week plan followed with weekly tuition, topic repair, cumulative retrieval, and sequenced exam practice.

Impact

Average topic performance rose from 48% to 72%, and the school mock moved from Grade 5 to Grade 7. The student became calmer, more secure, and more willing to engage with difficult material.

A-level Preparation

A-level Chemistry: turning effort into exam performance.

Weekly support for an Upper Sixth student whose paper scores were sitting around 59%.

Before 59%

Average paper score

After 76%

Average paper score

Result Secure A

Challenge

The student was revising consistently but not translating that effort into marks. Organic mechanisms, longer responses, and calculation-heavy papers were keeping them in low B territory.

Our approach

We completed a paper audit to identify whether the issue sat in knowledge, application, or exam method. The plan focused on precision under timed pressure, mark-scheme language, and a running error log reviewed each week.

Impact

Average paper scores improved from 59% to 76%, moving the student into secure A-grade performance. They became more disciplined in exam technique and more assured with difficult material.

Weekly Maths Support

Steady progress built through consistency and structure.

A two-term weekly arrangement for a Year 8 student needing sustained support rather than short-term intervention.

Before 61%

Average end-of-topic score

After 83%

Average end-of-topic score

Position At target

School performance by year end

Challenge

A capable Year 8 student had started to fall behind in algebraic reasoning and multi-step problem solving. Small gaps were beginning to compound and confidence had dipped.

Our approach

We reviewed school assessments, topic fluency, and exercise book patterns to map the exact points of slippage. A two-term weekly plan rebuilt fluency, strengthened algebra, and improved problem-solving method through sequenced teaching and spaced review.

Impact

Average topic performance rose from 61% to 83%, and the student moved from below target to securely at target. Confidence, classroom participation, and willingness to attempt harder problems all improved.
