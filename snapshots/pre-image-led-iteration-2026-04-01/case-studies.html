GCSE Maths

Rebuilding confidence and closing the core gaps first.

A 14-week programme for a Year 11 student whose average topic test score had fallen to 48% and whose working level had stalled at Grade 5.

Starting point 48%

Average topic test performance

Outcome 72%

Average topic score by the end of the plan

Exam shift 5 to 7

Mock grade movement before the exam period

Challenge

A Year 11 student at a high-performing school was working hard but underperforming in GCSE Maths. They had become anxious about the subject, were avoiding multi-step questions, and had visible gaps in algebra, ratio, and problem solving. Parents could see the effort, but progress was no longer following.

Our approach

We carried out a structured diagnostic review of recent test scripts, topic fluency, and exam habits. The diagnosis showed the issue was not ability but weak core recall, inconsistent method selection, and low confidence when questions became unfamiliar. A 14-week plan followed: weekly tuition, targeted topic repair, cumulative retrieval, sequenced exam practice, and fortnightly parent updates.

Impact

Average topic performance rose from 48% to 72%, and the student’s school mock moved from Grade 5 to Grade 7. More importantly, they became calmer with unfamiliar questions and much more secure in the topics that had previously caused them to freeze.

A-level Chemistry

Turning strong effort into exam performance.

Weekly support for an Upper Sixth student whose paper scores were sitting around 59% despite strong effort and ambitious university plans.

Starting point 59%

Average paper score before intervention

Outcome 76%

Average paper score later in the plan

Resulting level Secure A

Internal assessment standard by the end of the term

Challenge

This student was motivated and revising consistently, but they were not converting that effort into marks. Organic mechanisms, longer-response questions, and calculation-heavy papers were pulling results down and leaving them in low B territory.

Our approach

We carried out a detailed paper audit to diagnose whether the problem sat in knowledge, application, or exam method. The main issue turned out to be answer precision under time pressure rather than content coverage. We built a two-term plan around high-value topic repair, mark-scheme language, timed question drills, and a running error log reviewed each week.

Impact

Average paper scores improved from 59% to 76%, moving the student from uncertain B-grade work to secure A-grade performance. They became more disciplined in exam technique and more assured with difficult material heading into final assessments.

Weekly Maths Support

Steady progress through consistent structure.

A two-term weekly Maths arrangement for a Year 8 student who needed sustained structure rather than short-term intervention.

Starting point 61%

Average end-of-topic score before tuition

Outcome 83%

Average end-of-topic score by the end of the year

Position At target

Shift from below target to secure school performance

Challenge

A capable Year 8 student had started to fall behind in Maths, particularly in algebraic reasoning and multi-step problem solving. End-of-topic results were averaging 61%, confidence had dipped, and parents were concerned that small gaps were beginning to compound.

Our approach

We reviewed school assessments, exercise-book patterns, and topic fluency to pinpoint where understanding was slipping. A two-term weekly plan then rebuilt number fluency, strengthened algebra, and improved problem-solving method through sequenced tuition, spaced review, and close alignment with school content.

Impact

Average end-of-topic performance rose from 61% to 83%, and the student moved from below target to securely at target. Just as importantly, they became more willing to attempt unfamiliar problems and far more confident week to week.
