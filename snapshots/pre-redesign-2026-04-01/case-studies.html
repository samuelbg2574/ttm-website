GCSE support

GCSE Maths: rebuilding confidence and closing core gaps - Grade 5 to Grade 7

A 14-week GCSE Maths programme for a Year 11 student whose average test score had fallen to 48% and whose working level was stuck at Grade 5.

Challenge

A Year 11 student at a high-performing school was working hard but underperforming in GCSE Maths. They had become anxious about the subject, were avoiding multi-step questions, and had clear gaps in algebra, ratio, and problem solving. Parents could see effort was there, but progress was not following.

Our approach

We began with a structured diagnostic review of recent test scripts, topic fluency, and exam habits to identify the root causes of difficulty. This showed that the issue was not ability but weak core recall, inconsistent method selection, and low confidence when questions became unfamiliar. A tailored 14-week plan was then put in place: weekly tuition, targeted repair of core topics, cumulative retrieval, carefully sequenced exam-style practice, and fortnightly parent feedback outlining what had been covered, where progress was being made, and what would come next.

Impact

Average topic test performance rose from 48% to 72%, and the student’s school mock improved from Grade 5 to Grade 7. Just as importantly, they became calmer with unfamiliar questions, more secure in foundational topics, and noticeably more confident approaching the exam period.

A-level preparation

A-level Chemistry: turning effort into exam performance

Weekly A-level Chemistry support for an Upper Sixth student whose paper scores were sitting around 59% despite strong effort and high university ambitions.

Challenge

This student was highly motivated and revising consistently, but was not converting that effort into marks. They were losing points on organic mechanisms, longer-response questions, and calculation-heavy papers, which left them in low B territory and increasingly frustrated.

Our approach

We carried out a detailed paper audit to diagnose whether the problem sat in knowledge, application, or exam method. The diagnosis showed that the main issue was not content coverage but weak answer precision under time pressure. We designed a two-term plan built around high-value topic repair, mark-scheme language, timed question drills, and a running error log reviewed each week. Delivery stayed consistent throughout the term, with clear half-term parent updates summarising progress, readiness, and next priorities.

Impact

Average paper scores improved from 59% to 76%, and internal assessment moved from B-grade uncertainty to secure A-grade performance. The student became more disciplined in exam technique, more confident with difficult material, and much more assured heading into final assessments.

Ongoing weekly tuition

Weekly Maths support: steady progress through consistency

A two-term weekly Maths arrangement for a Year 8 student who needed sustained structure rather than short-term intervention.

Challenge

A capable Year 8 student had started to fall behind in Maths, particularly in algebraic reasoning and multi-step problem solving. End-of-topic results were averaging 61%, classroom confidence had dipped, and parents were concerned that small gaps were beginning to compound.

Our approach

We began with a structured review of school assessments, exercise-book patterns, and topic fluency to diagnose the exact points where understanding was slipping. A two-term weekly plan was then mapped out to rebuild number fluency, strengthen algebra, and improve problem-solving method through sequenced tuition, spaced review, and close alignment with school content. Parents received monthly updates covering progress, current focus, and where greater confidence was beginning to show.

Impact

Average end-of-topic performance rose from 61% to 83%, and the student moved from below target to securely at target by the end of the year. More importantly, they became more willing to attempt unfamiliar problems, more active in class, and far more confident in the subject week to week.
